# Strategic Analysis of the NGI Zero Commons Fund

## Executive Summary
The NGI Zero (NGI0) Commons Fund is a specialized financial vehicle managed by checking the NLnet Foundation that operates under the Horizon Europe framework. It aims to deliver, mature, and scale "internet commons" as free and open-source software (FOSS) or open hardware.

## Strategic Foundations
- **Internet Commons**: Resources collectively owned or shared (code, protocols, standards).
- **Full-Stack Philosophy**: Support for Libre Silicon, Middleware, P2P Infrastructure, and End-User Applications.
- **Geopolitical Goals**: Alignment with EU's Digital Agenda and Digital Sovereignty.

## Fund Architecture (Cascade Funding)
- **Budget**: ~21.6 Million Euro.
- **Tiered Limits**:
    - **50,000 Euro**: Entry tier for first proposals.
    - **150,000 Euro**: Growth tier for established projects.
    - **500,000 Euro**: Lifetime cap per entity.
- **Timeline**: 3-5 months for review; 1-12 months for execution.

## Operational Mechanics
- **Financial Model**: Donation-based (VAT exempt).
- **Payment**: Retrospective milestone-based (no advance payments).
- **Eligibility**: Open to individuals, non-profits, academia, and corporations.
- **European Dimension**: Required (60% EU funds or significant strategic value to Europe).

## Evaluation Criteria (Weighted)
1. **Relevance / Impact (40%)**: Strategic fit and breakthrough potential.
2. **Technical Excellence (30%)**: Architecture and expertise.
3. **Cost Effectiveness (30%)**: Budget justification.

## Compliance Mandates
- **Open Source**: Mandatory (FOSS).
- **Accessibility**: WCAG compliance required for scaling beyond 50k.
- **Security**: Mandatory independent security audits for projects >50k.
- **Standardization**: Encouragement of participation in standards bodies.
